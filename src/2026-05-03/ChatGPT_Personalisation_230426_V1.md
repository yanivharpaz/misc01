## Slide 2: What is the purpose of Personalisation in ChatGPT

Think of Personalisation as **how ChatGPT adapts to *you*** over time.

Its purpose is to:

- Remember preferences, such as tone, format, interests, and habits.
- Adjust how it responds, for example by being more concise, more structured, or more strategic.
- Build continuity across conversations.

It is not about what gets done, but **how it is done for you**.

**Example:** If you prefer structured, business-oriented answers, Personalisation nudges responses in that direction automatically.

GPTs are about **creating a tailored version of ChatGPT for a specific role or task**.

Their purpose is to:

- Encapsulate instructions, knowledge, and tools into a reusable “agent”.
- Be used repeatedly for a defined function, such as proposal writer, fitness coach, or legal explainer.

They are:

- Task-focused.
- Reusable across contexts.
- Shareable with others.

**Example:** A “Solution Architect GPT” that always structures answers like a client proposal.

In the scheme of tailoring ChatGPT:

- **Personalisation** → adapts to you.
- **GPTs** → predefined roles.
- **Skills** → specific capabilities/actions.
- **Projects** → organised workspaces.

## Slide 3: Initial Settings

1. Open ChatGPT.
2. Right click on **Oracle Corporation**.
3. Select the drop down for:
   - Base style and tone.
   - Characteristics, i.e. More, Default or Less.
   - Custom instructions.
4. Add your:
   - Nickname.
   - Occupation.
   - More about you.
5. Leave **Memory** and **Advanced** as default.

## Slide 4: Writing the ‘More about you’

1. Create a new chat.
2. Copy the following prompt:

> Help me write a good 'More about you' section for my personization. Could you ask me questions to get all the information you need, then write it please.

3. Answer all the questions it provides and respond to any follow-on questions that are asked.
4. Review the ChatGPT output to see if it accurately reflects you.
5. Alter as appropriate. Do this with ChatGPT, as it will start to learn more about you.
6. Copy and paste the final version into the **More about you** section back in personization.

As you use the tool more, you might want to tweak this to get better outputs from ChatGPT. For example, review this section when you find yourself thinking:

- This is too high-level.
- This is overcomplicated.
- This is not how I think about problems.

## Slide 5: The “More about you” section in preferences

This section should lean more toward your working style, rather than what you do.

For Oracle, this section is limited to 1,500 characters.

### Questions that ChatGPT tends to ask when helping optimise this section

#### 1. Is your use of ChatGPT mostly:

- Work only.
- Mainly work, some personal.
- Mixed evenly.

#### 2. When helping you with work, what should ChatGPT optimise for first?

Pick one or rank them:

- Customer value.
- Commercial viability.
- Delivery realism.
- Stakeholder alignment.
- Balanced trade-offs across all four.

#### 3. What response structure do you prefer by default?

- Short executive summary first, then detail.
- Concise bullets only.
- Structured sections.
- Prose/narrative.
- Options with pros and cons.

#### 4. How much challenge do you want?

- Mostly support and refinement.
- Constructive pushback.
- Active stress-testing of assumptions.
- Strong challenge when logic, risk, or delivery reality is weak.

#### 5. What should ChatGPT include by default when helping on work topics?

Choose any that matter:

- Assumptions.
- Risks.
- Dependencies.
- Gaps in information.
- Stakeholder impacts.
- Recommended next steps.
- Commercial considerations.
- Delivery implications.

## Slide 7: ‘More About You’ examples

I am a Service Solutions Architect in Oracle Customer Success Services (CSS), working between sales, the customer, and delivery to shape service solutions that are valuable, commercially viable, and deliverable. I use ChatGPT for work only. Use UK English and a professional, polished tone. Start with a short executive summary, then provide structured detail. Use bullets or numbered lists where helpful. Present options with relevant pros and cons. Keep responses practical, fact-based, and free of fluffy consulting language or unnecessary complexity. Give constructive pushback where needed. When drafting documents or presentations, ask about the audience and whether to prioritise customer value, commercial viability, delivery realism, stakeholder alignment, or a balanced trade-off where it is not clear. Include assumptions, risks, dependencies, stakeholder impacts, next steps, commercial considerations, and delivery implications where relevant. Spell out acronyms on first use and assume only light technical knowledge in the reader.

### What do I do?

My role essentially comprises four core aspects:

- Understanding the customer’s business goals, IT landscape, and service requirements.
- Turning those needs into a scoped, risk-aware, costed CSS service solution.
- Coordinating with sales, Oracle xLoB teams, delivery SMEs, partners, and commercial/contracts stakeholders around that solution.
- Helping to win the deal and then handing over into delivery.

## Slide 9: What is the purpose of Skills in ChatGPT

Skills are about **capabilities or workflows ChatGPT can execute**.

Their purpose is to:

- Perform specific actions or processes, often multi-step.
- Extend what ChatGPT can *do*, not just how it talks.

They are:

- More operational.
- Sometimes tool-driven, for example analysing files, running code, or structured workflows.

**Example:** A skill that analyses a CSV and produces insights, or runs a structured evaluation process.

---

## Slide 10: What is the purpose of Projects in ChatGPT

Projects are about organising work and context over time.

Their purpose is to:

- Group conversations, files, and outputs around a specific initiative.
- Maintain context for ongoing work.

They are:

- Context containers.
- Useful for multi-session efforts.

**Example:** A “Customer Transformation Deal” project with all related chats, docs, and iterations.
